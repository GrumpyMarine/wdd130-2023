Overview

Purpose

We are a local non profit garden club who focuses on teaching, planting, growing and selling iris and daylily flowers. Our goal is to educate people of all ages of the beauty of these drought tollerant flowers. We are affiliated with The American Iris Society.

Audience

We are focusing our webpage towards those who already have a green thumb, to those lacking and most importantly to those who would like to learn how to garden in the Hi Desert. Our goal is to reach out too our senior citizens, mom and dads, teenagers and youth, by creating a stronger community while learning how to plant, grow and sell flowers.

Home page

Who We Are:

Hi-Desert Iris and Daylily is a 503(c)(3) non-profit corporation and an educational organization with a goal of promoting Iris and Daylily as a drought-tolerant plant suitable for growing in a desert environment. We are an affiliate of the American Iris Society, Region 15 and founded April 1988. We live in a desert surrounded by dry to dead plants and lots of dirt. With the education and teamwork of our organization, we would like to show others the beauty of the desert when you add the Irises and Daylily’s.

Purpose:

The website's purpose is to create awareness of the Hi-Desert Iris and Daylily Society and to build and increase friendships amongst members of the community. We plan to use this website to bridge the social gap between the youth, young adults, mature adults, and senior citizens. We will be using the website to publish a membership directory, upcoming meetings, social events to include planting, growing, and selling. This website will benefit all levels of society by creating a place where people can get together and never feel alone.

Calendar:

Inside the Calendar, you will have access to upcoming meetings to include dates, start times, and locations. A second option will be social events to include planting parties, growing classes, and fundraising events by selling Irises and Daylily's.

Planting and Zones

Planting:

In this section, we will discuss the different zones, and show a map of the zones. Depending on your climate zone, this will determine what types of Irises and Daylily’s can be grown. Each zone differs based on the average annual minimum winter temperature, divided into 10-degree F zones, and further divided into 5-degree F half-zones. We will discuss how to plant the rhizome (bulb), maintain the flower, and replant from an existing plant. We will discuss the various parts of the plant.

Types and Parts of the Iris:

We will discuss the diverse types of Irises and Daylily’s. There are regular, historic, and then custom types and names. There are about 280 varied species of Irises in the world. Many of them are native to Europe and Asia. Within the United States there are 28 native species. We will also identify the different parts of an Iris.

Membership

Directory:

Inside the directory we will list members' names and positions within the club. By joining the Hi-Desert Iris and Daylily Society, you will not only become a member, but a part of our family. You will have access to other members, who can help you with your gardening questions and in turn assist someone else, paying it forward.

Committee:

Within the committee we have the following offices: President, Vice President, Treasurer and Secretary. Then we have the committee chairs who oversee the following: The nursey, Historian, Hospitality, Media/Publicity, Membership, Name badges, Newsletter/Emails, Nursery Manager, Rhizome Rubles, and Telephones calls.

Membership:

Our membership is $15 for an individual, $20 for couples, and $5 for youth under 18. The dues are due annually. Our meetings are held on the third Saturday of each month. We hold horticultural and artistic shows in April and May.
